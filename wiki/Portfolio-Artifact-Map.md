# Portfolio Artifact Map

This map translates the portfolio into artifact categories that hiring managers, recruiters, PMO leaders, portfolio leaders, and AI operations teams are more likely to recognize.

For the shortest public route, start at [policani.net](https://policani.net). For concrete walkthroughs, use the portfolio journeys:

- [Tradeoffs to executive decision route](https://policani.net/journeys/tradeoffs-to-executive-decision-route.html)

## Governance frameworks

Frameworks for structuring demand, decision rights, prioritization, delivery readiness, value, controls, and follow-through.

Representative public-safe examples:

- PMO and PPMO formation logic
- Portfolio intake and readiness triage
- Prioritization and scoring criteria
- AI opportunity review and reliance boundaries
- Controls, exposure, and value realization governance

## Operating models

Models for how work should move through an environment: who owns what, what evidence is needed, what cadence applies, what decisions are required, and what should happen next.

Representative public-safe examples:

- Executive operating cadence
- Portfolio sequencing and capacity visibility
- Partner ecosystem governance
- Delivery readiness and UAT governance
- Evidence-bound workflow architecture

## Playbooks and templates

Reusable routines, prompts, checklists, and output structures for turning messy work into decision-ready artifacts.

Representative public-safe examples:

- Business case preparation
- Project charter initiation
- Executive review packs
- Governance operations logs
- Release readiness and UAT packs

## Lessons learned

Generalized lessons from environments where activity was visible but not yet governable.

Representative themes:

- Signal quality matters before prioritization.
- Governance should make work easier to steer, not harder to do.
- AI should improve structure and review coverage without taking over accountability.
- Public examples need enough specificity to show judgment while protecting confidential source details.

## Pattern library

The [operating-patterns](https://github.com/policani/operating-patterns) repository is the source bundle behind the pattern layer. It is intentionally anonymized and portable. The pattern format is a privacy-safe way to show how real operating problems were structured without turning confidential work into public case studies.
