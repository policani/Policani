# Portfolio Decision Readiness Rubric

| Dimension | Ready | Needs work | Not ready |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sponsor ownership | Every initiative has an accountable sponsor. | A few sponsors are missing or unclear. | Many initiatives lack sponsors. |
| Delivery ownership | Every initiative has a named owner. | Some ownership gaps exist. | Ownership is broadly unclear. |
| Decision authority | Approval body or authority is clear. | Some authority gaps exist. | Decisions cannot be made confidently. |
| Cost and capacity | Estimates are visible and directionally credible. | Estimates are incomplete or rough. | Portfolio cannot be evaluated for affordability. |
| Benefit clarity | Benefits are stated and tied to strategy. | Benefits are broad or weakly supported. | Benefits are unclear or absent. |
| Mandatory status | Mandatory, committed, discretionary, or unknown status is clear. | Some status ambiguity remains. | Mandatory and discretionary work are mixed. |
| Risk and dependency visibility | Major risks and dependencies are visible. | Some key risks or dependencies may be missing. | Cross-initiative exposure is unknown. |
| Scenario readiness | At least one useful scenario can be tested. | Scenarios are possible but weak. | Scenario analysis would be misleading. |
| Executive brief quality | Leaders can make a decision from the brief. | Brief supports discussion but needs cleanup. | Brief is not decision-ready. |
