# Intake and Root-Cause Challenge

## Purpose

Do not let the user build the wrong business case. Many requests arrive as solution statements: "We need a new tool," "We need more headcount," or "Teams are not aligned." Treat these as hypotheses, not facts.

## Pre-parse the input

Extract what is already known across five dimensions:

1. Problem clarity
2. Organizational context
3. Financial parameters
4. Constraints and prior art
5. Audience and decision purpose

Only ask for missing information. Do not re-ask what the user already gave you.

## Root-cause challenge

Challenge diagnoses that may be symptoms:

- "We are at capacity" may mean WIP is uncontrolled.
- "The tool does not work" may mean process discipline is weak.
- "Teams are not aligned" may mean decision rights are unclear.
- "We need more resources" may mean prioritization is failing.

Ask one targeted root-cause probe when the diagnosis is not yet credible.

## Question cap

Ask no more than 8 questions in one batch. Group by dimension.

## Confirmation brief

Before drafting, confirm:

- Problem
- Stakes
- Scope
- Audience and decision
- Key constraints
- Assumptions carried forward
