# Case Study - Refund Error Reduction

Purpose: Use for work involving revenue, cost, budget, customer impact, risk reduction, compliance, partner ecosystem, product/platform scale, or executive decision support.

## Usage gate

Use real figures only. If the number is directional, write `estimated` and explain the basis.

## Purpose

Use for: This story helps prove customer issue review, error tracking, operations follow-up, risk reduction, and clear communication.

## Context

At Example Retail Group in 2021, the customer support team was seeing repeated refund mistakes. Customers were frustrated, support agents were spending extra time fixing issues, and managers needed to know whether the problem was a training issue, system issue, or process issue.

## Scale

- Refund issues appeared in about 35 customer cases over one month.
- 2 support teams and 1 operations lead were involved.
- The issue affected customer trust, support time, and refund accuracy.

## Problem

Some refunds were being entered with the wrong reason code or missing notes. This made it harder for operations to see why refunds were happening and created extra follow-up for customers and support agents.

## Constraints

- The support team could not change the refund system directly.
- Customer cases still had to be handled the same day when possible.
- Managers needed examples before asking operations to change the process.

## Your role

I was a customer support representative. I did not own the refund system or approve process changes, but I could identify repeated problems, document examples, and share clear notes with my lead.

## Actions

- Tracked refund cases that had missing notes or wrong reason codes.
- Grouped examples by issue type so the pattern was easier to see.
- Shared a short summary with my team lead and the operations lead.
- Helped update the support checklist so agents knew which notes were required before submitting a refund.

## Outcomes

- Identified about 35 refund cases with repeated documentation problems in one month.
- Helped the team update the refund checklist used by support agents.
- Reduced repeated follow-up questions because refund notes became clearer.

## Evidence quality

- Measured: count of refund cases reviewed during the month.
- Observed: fewer repeat questions from operations after the checklist was updated.
- Reasonable conclusion: refund review became easier because support agents had clearer note expectations.

## Employer-facing summary

Identified repeated refund documentation problems by tracking customer cases, grouping examples, and sharing a clear summary with team and operations leads. Helped update the support checklist so refund notes were more complete and follow-up questions were reduced.

## Guardrails

- Do not claim ownership of the refund system.
- Do not claim final approval authority for process changes.
- Do not claim a dollar savings amount unless a verified savings figure exists.
