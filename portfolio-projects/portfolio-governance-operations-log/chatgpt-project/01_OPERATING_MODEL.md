# Operating Model

The Portfolio Governance Operations Log is the operating layer around recurring PMO governance work. It is designed for the work a PMO, portfolio manager, governance lead, chief of staff, or program leader does between formal systems and formal meetings.

## Core loop

1. Capture rough signals during the week.
2. Normalize the signals into a structured worklog.
3. Classify the signals into governance categories.
4. Separate routine status from material decisions, risks, blockers, dependencies, and follow-up.
5. Prepare the meeting.
6. Facilitate decisions and commitments.
7. Close the meeting.
8. Carry unresolved items into the next cycle.

## Governance categories

- Routine status
- Decision needed
- Action item
- Follow-up required
- Risk
- Issue
- Blocker
- Dependency
- Escalation candidate
- Executive air-support request
- Scheduling / rescheduling need
- Project-plan update
- Weak signal
- Missing status
- Stale update
- Carry-forward item

## PMO value

The system improves governance hygiene by making hidden operating signals visible: weak updates, stale claims, missing owners, unresolved dependencies, unclear decisions, and follow-through gaps.

## Human accountability

The AI supports interpretation and drafting. People retain authority for decisions, commitments, approvals, risk acceptance, funding, sequencing, owner changes, and escalation direction.
