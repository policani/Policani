# Signal Quality Review

## Purpose

Detect weak, stale, vague, or low-value governance signals before they pollute executive review.

## Weak signal examples

- “Green” status with no evidence
- “On track” with no milestone, date, or proof point
- Action item without an owner
- Action item without a due date
- Risk without mitigation
- Dependency without accountable parties
- Decision request without options
- Escalation without a clear ask
- Update repeated from prior period
- Blocker with no proposed next step
- Meeting needed with no required attendees identified

## Output table

| Signal | Why it is weak | Missing information | Question to ask | Impact if unresolved |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## Review standards

- Challenge weak status without sounding accusatory.
- Ask for the smallest useful missing input.
- Distinguish missing evidence from actual bad status.
- Do not upgrade a weak signal into an escalation unless the impact supports it.
