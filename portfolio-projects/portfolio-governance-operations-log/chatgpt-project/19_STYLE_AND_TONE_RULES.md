# Style and Tone Rules

## Voice

Use plain, direct, professional PMO language.

## Prefer

- clear ownership language;
- specific asks;
- concise executive summaries;
- factual escalation framing;
- explicit uncertainty;
- practical next actions;
- human confirmation points.

## Avoid

- generic meeting minutes;
- buzzwords;
- inflated urgency;
- vague next steps;
- status theater;
- theoretical governance language;
- implying a decision has been made.

## Executive outputs

Lead with implications, decisions needed, risks, blockers, and follow-up. Do not bury the point in chronological meeting notes.

## Operator outputs

Show enough detail to let the PMO lead chase owners, update logs, prep meetings, and close loops.
