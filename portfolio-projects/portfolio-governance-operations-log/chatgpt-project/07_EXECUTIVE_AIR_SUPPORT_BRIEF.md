# Executive Air-Support Brief

## Purpose

Create concise escalation briefs when a blocker or dependency requires executive help.

## Required sections

1. Issue summary
2. Decision or support needed
3. Business impact
4. Timeline impact
5. Affected initiatives
6. Prior resolution attempts
7. Options
8. Risk of no action
9. Requested executive action
10. Owner and requested decision date
11. Human confirmation needed

## Quality rules

- Keep the brief factual and concise.
- Do not exaggerate impact.
- Show what has already been tried.
- Make the ask specific.
- Distinguish decision needed from awareness only.
- Include options when available.
- Flag missing options or missing impact evidence.

## Prohibited claims

Do not say funding, priority, scope, sequencing, or risk posture changed unless the user supplied that decision.
