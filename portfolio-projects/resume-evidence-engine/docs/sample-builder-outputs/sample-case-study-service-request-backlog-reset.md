# Case Study - Service Request Backlog Reset

Purpose: Use for a major project, program, transformation, delivery recovery, governance build, launch, or operational reset.

## Usage gate

Complete this only with real facts. Do not invent metrics. Mark estimates as estimates.

## Purpose

Use for: This story helps prove operations, customer support, request tracking, team coordination, process improvement, and simple reporting.

## Context

In 2024, Example Customer Services had a customer operations team with more open service requests than the old tracking process could handle. Customers were asking for updates, team leads were using different lists, and managers did not have one clear view of the work.

## Stakes and constraints

- 180 open service requests needed review or follow-up.
- 3 teams were tracking requests in different formats.
- Managers needed a clear update before the next monthly review.
- The team needed a fix that could work without buying a new system.

## Problem

The obvious problem was a large request backlog. The deeper problem was that requests did not always have a clear owner, priority, status, or next step.

## Options considered

### Option 1 - Keep the old tracker

- Pros: No new process to teach.
- Cons: The same ownership and visibility problems would continue.

### Option 2 - Wait for a new tool

- Pros: A new tool might work better later.
- Cons: It would take too long and would not help the current backlog.

### Option 3 - Standardize the tracker and review it weekly

- Pros: Fast, simple, and easy for team leads to use.
- Cons/tradeoff: Team leads had to keep the tracker updated each week.

## Decision and rationale

I chose a simple shared tracker and weekly review routine because it solved the immediate visibility problem without waiting for a new system.

## Execution

- I sorted requests by customer group, owner, age, status, and next step.
- I created a shared tracker so team leads could see the same list.
- I set up a weekly review to close old requests, update blockers, and assign next steps.
- I wrote short intake rules so new requests had enough detail to route correctly.

## Outcomes

- Reduced open service requests from 180 to 42 in six weeks.
- Gave managers one weekly view of request owners, blockers, and overdue items.
- Reduced repeated status questions because teams could see current request notes.

## What changed

Before the reset, requests lived in scattered notes and separate team lists. After the reset, each request had an owner, priority, status, blocker if any, and next step.

## Why it mattered

Customers received clearer updates, team leads could focus on the oldest and highest-risk requests, and managers could see where help was needed.
