# Business Case Maturity Review

**Score:** 56/60
**Readiness:** Decision-ready with caveats.

## Top Improvements

- Problem definition: Define the business problem in operational terms: who is affected, what pain exists, how often it occurs, and why it matters now.
- Strategic alignment: Tie the initiative to named strategy, objectives, regulatory obligations, customer outcomes, cost discipline, or risk reduction.
- Recommendation quality: Explain why the recommended path is better than the alternatives under the stated constraints.
- Cost and funding clarity: Separate one-time implementation costs from recurring operating costs and identify funding source where possible.
- Decision clarity: State the approval action, decision owner, timing, and what happens immediately after approval or rejection.

## Criterion Detail

### Decision clarity: 5/5

**Issue:** The case must make the requested leadership decision explicit.

**Counsel:** State the approval action, decision owner, timing, and what happens immediately after approval or rejection.

**Evidence needed:** Decision body, approval threshold, requested funding or authorization, and decision date.

### Problem definition: 4/5

**Issue:** A vague problem statement makes every downstream option look arbitrary.

**Counsel:** Define the business problem in operational terms: who is affected, what pain exists, how often it occurs, and why it matters now.

**Evidence needed:** Current-state examples, incidents, lost time, cost, revenue leakage, risk exposure, or stakeholder feedback.

### Strategic alignment: 4/5

**Issue:** The case needs to explain why this initiative belongs in the portfolio.

**Counsel:** Tie the initiative to named strategy, objectives, regulatory obligations, customer outcomes, cost discipline, or risk reduction.

**Evidence needed:** Strategic plan excerpts, OKRs, annual priorities, compliance drivers, customer commitments, or portfolio themes.

### Current-state evidence: 5/5

**Issue:** Leadership will challenge the case if the pain is asserted but not evidenced.

**Counsel:** Quantify current-state pain where possible and label any estimates as assumptions.

**Evidence needed:** Baseline metrics, process data, financials, ticket volumes, cycle time, error rates, support load, risk events, or qualitative evidence.

### Options analysis: 5/5

**Issue:** A single-solution case reads like advocacy rather than decision analysis.

**Counsel:** Compare at least three options, including do nothing, with clear tradeoffs.

**Evidence needed:** Option costs, benefits, feasibility, time-to-value, dependency, and risk notes.

### Recommendation quality: 4/5

**Issue:** The recommendation must follow from the option analysis.

**Counsel:** Explain why the recommended path is better than the alternatives under the stated constraints.

**Evidence needed:** Selection criteria, weighted tradeoffs, stakeholder input, feasibility assessment, and risk-adjusted value.

### Value case: 5/5

**Issue:** Benefits must be credible enough to justify the investment or change.

**Counsel:** Separate hard financial benefits from qualitative, risk, compliance, productivity, and customer benefits.

**Evidence needed:** Benefit owner, baseline, target, measurement method, confidence level, and validation plan.

### Cost and funding clarity: 4/5

**Issue:** Missing or vague costs make approval fragile.

**Counsel:** Separate one-time implementation costs from recurring operating costs and identify funding source where possible.

**Evidence needed:** Labor, vendor, platform, training, support, change management, licensing, integration, and run-rate costs.

### Risk and constraint discipline: 5/5

**Issue:** Risks, assumptions, and constraints should not be buried or softened.

**Counsel:** Make the risk register decision-useful: impact, likelihood, mitigation, owner, and validation trigger.

**Evidence needed:** Risk log, dependency map, compliance review, security review, vendor constraints, resource constraints, and assumption tests.

### Implementation feasibility: 5/5

**Issue:** The business case does not need a full project plan, but it needs a credible path.

**Counsel:** Show phases, milestones, owners, dependencies, and governance checkpoints.

**Evidence needed:** Milestone plan, owner list, dependency map, delivery estimate, governance cadence, and readiness criteria.

### Evidence traceability: 5/5

**Issue:** The case should distinguish what is known from what must still be validated.

**Counsel:** Add an evidence appendix, identify source artifacts, and list open questions that could change the recommendation.

**Evidence needed:** Named evidence artifacts, source notes, assumptions log, and unresolved decision questions.

### Executive readability: 5/5

**Issue:** A business case must be usable by a busy decision-maker.

**Counsel:** Keep the summary short, put the decision request near the front, and use tables for tradeoffs, risks, costs, and next steps.

**Evidence needed:** Executive summary, decision table, option comparison, cost/benefit tables, and next-step owner list.
