# Business Case Maturity Review

**Score:** 35/35
**Readiness:** Executive-ready. Suitable for leadership review with normal validation.

## Top Improvements

- Problem clarity: Tighten the business problem, name observable symptoms, separate symptom from root cause, and quantify the cost of inaction.
- Evidence grounding: Label assumptions, name evidence limitations, and connect each major number to a basis or source.
- Options integrity: Evaluate Do Nothing and credible alternatives against consistent criteria. Do not strawman options.

## Quality Rubric

| Dimension | Score | Finding | Counsel | Evidence Needed |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Problem clarity | 5/5 | Tests whether the problem is stated in business terms, separated from root cause, scoped, and quantified. | Tighten the business problem, name observable symptoms, separate symptom from root cause, and quantify the cost of inaction. | Observable symptoms, root-cause evidence, scope boundaries, urgency driver, and status quo impact. |
| Evidence grounding | 5/5 | Tests whether claims are supported by data, source artifacts, or clearly stated assumptions. | Label assumptions, name evidence limitations, and connect each major number to a basis or source. | Baseline metrics, source inventory, assumption log, data limitations, and validation plan. |
| Options integrity | 5/5 | Tests whether the recommendation follows from real alternatives rather than a predetermined solution. | Evaluate Do Nothing and credible alternatives against consistent criteria. Do not strawman options. | Option costs, timelines, benefits, tradeoffs, scores, and scoring rationale. |
| Financial rigor | 5/5 | Tests cost completeness, hard/soft benefit separation, return analysis, assumptions, and sensitivity. | Separate hard benefits from soft benefits, show Year 1 cost, annual hard benefit, payback/ROI, and downside scenarios. | Cost categories, benefit basis, ROI/payback method, assumptions, sensitivity scenarios. |
| Risk honesty | 5/5 | Tests whether material risks are named, assessed, mitigated, and owned. | Use named failure modes, likelihood, impact, severity, mitigation, residual risk, and owner. | Risk register, mitigation plan, owner list, dependency map, compliance/security review if applicable. |
| Executive readability | 5/5 | Tests whether a time-pressed executive can understand the problem, recommendation, cost, return, risk, and ask quickly. | Put the decision request near the front, keep the executive summary self-contained, and use tables for financials, risks, and options. | Standalone executive summary, decision table, concise recommendation, readable financial and risk tables. |
| Decision clarity | 5/5 | Tests whether the document asks for a specific decision from a specific decision-maker by a specific date. | State the named decision, owner, deadline, consequence of delay, and first actions if approved. | Decision owner, approval action, date, consequence of delay, next-step owner list. |
