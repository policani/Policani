# Business Case Playbook

## Mental model

Current state → gap → root cause → options → recommendation → value → risk → decision.

## When to build a business case

Build one when an idea needs approval, funding, prioritization, or governance sign-off, especially when multiple paths are possible or the current state has a meaningful cost of inaction.

## Minimum evidence before drafting

- Business problem or opportunity.
- Strategic objective supported.
- Current-state pain.
- Desired future state.
- Key stakeholders.
- Rough scope and timing.
- Enough evidence to estimate costs, benefits, alternatives, risks, and assumptions.

## What approval feeds

If approved, the business case feeds project initiation, chartering, funding approval, portfolio prioritization, detailed planning, and requirements work. It also becomes the baseline for later governance: are we still aligned to the value proposition that justified the work?
