# Audience Framing

A business case is aimed at a decision-maker. The facts should remain stable, but the emphasis should shift based on audience.

## CFO / Financial Authority

Emphasize ROI, payback, cost completeness, hard vs. soft benefits, sensitivity, and opportunity cost.

Likely challenge: “Where are the assumptions, and what breaks the model?”

## CEO / Executive Sponsor

Emphasize strategic stakes, organizational risk, timing, competitive position, and reversibility.

Likely challenge: “Why now, and what happens if this does not work?”

## Operational / Department Head

Emphasize resource load, implementation feasibility, change burden, ownership, and interaction with existing commitments.

Likely challenge: “Can my team absorb this without breaking current delivery?”

## IT / Technology Leadership

Emphasize architecture, integration, security, migration, data quality, support model, vendor dependency, and technical debt.

Likely challenge: “Are you underestimating integration and production support?”

## Interview Panel

Emphasize structured thinking, assumptions, stakeholder awareness, directional financial fluency, risk honesty, and a clear recommendation.

Likely challenge: “Can you land a defensible recommendation under ambiguity?”

## Multi-audience default

When the audience is mixed or unknown, use CFO-grade financial rigor, executive-level strategic framing, and operational-level implementation specificity.
