# Critical Review Council

The review council is a set of professional review lenses. It prevents the business case from being written only from the author’s preferred perspective.

## Executive sponsor lens

Tests whether the decision is clear.

Questions:

- What exactly is leadership being asked to approve?
- Is this funding, discovery, prioritization, scope change, or cancellation?
- What happens next if approved?
- What happens if rejected or deferred?

## Finance lens

Tests whether the money story is credible.

Questions:

- Are costs visible?
- Are benefits measurable or honestly qualitative?
- Are ROI claims supported?
- Are one-time and ongoing costs separated?
- What assumptions drive the financial case?

## Operations lens

Tests whether the organization can absorb the change.

Questions:

- Who must change behavior?
- What process, support, staffing, or training changes are needed?
- What operational risks are being created?
- What handoffs or ownership boundaries matter?

## Technology and engineering lens

Tests feasibility.

Questions:

- What systems, data, integrations, security, reliability, or technical debt matter?
- What dependencies could block delivery?
- Is the solution over-specified before discovery?
- Are engineering estimates labeled as assumptions where needed?

## Delivery lens

Tests execution realism.

Questions:

- Are milestones credible?
- Are owners named?
- Are dependencies explicit?
- Is governance built into the path?
- What is the minimum viable decision path?

## Risk and compliance lens

Tests exposure.

Questions:

- What could materially alter or invalidate the case?
- What regulatory, security, contractual, financial, operational, or reputational risks exist?
- What assumptions need validation before full commitment?

## Customer or user lens

Tests impact.

Questions:

- Who benefits?
- How will they experience the change?
- What pain is removed?
- What new burden might be introduced?
- How will adoption or satisfaction be measured?
