# Business Case Maturity Model

The maturity model scores the case for decision readiness, not writing quality alone.

Each criterion receives a 0-5 score.

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 0 | Missing or unusable |
| 1 | Present but vague |
| 2 | Directionally useful, weak evidence |
| 3 | Adequate for early review |
| 4 | Strong enough for decision discussion |
| 5 | Leadership-ready and evidence-grounded |

## Criteria

1. **Decision clarity**: The requested decision is explicit.
2. **Problem definition**: The problem or opportunity is specific and material.
3. **Strategic alignment**: The case explains why this matters now.
4. **Current-state evidence**: Pain, waste, risk, or opportunity cost are supported.
5. **Options analysis**: Alternatives are fairly compared, including do nothing.
6. **Recommendation quality**: The recommended path follows from the analysis.
7. **Value case**: Benefits are credible, measurable where possible, and tied to stakeholders.
8. **Cost and funding clarity**: Costs, investment needs, and financial assumptions are visible.
9. **Risk and constraint discipline**: Risks, assumptions, dependencies, and constraints are explicit.
10. **Implementation feasibility**: Timeline, ownership, milestones, and governance are credible.
11. **Evidence traceability**: Claims can be traced to artifacts, facts, or labeled assumptions.
12. **Executive readability**: The document is concise, structured, and decision-oriented.

## Readiness bands

| Score | Readiness |
|---:|---|
| 0-29 | Not ready. Use for discovery only. |
| 30-44 | Early draft. Major gaps remain. |
| 45-54 | Reviewable. Needs targeted strengthening. |
| 55-64 | Decision-ready with caveats. |
| 65-72 | Strong leadership-ready case. |

## Critical counsel rules

A score is not enough. Every low score must produce counsel:

- What is weak
- Why it matters
- How to fix it
- What evidence is needed
- How the gap affects the decision
