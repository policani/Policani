# Maturity Model

The maturity review is a professional counsel layer. It is not a copy edit. It tests whether the business case is ready to withstand sponsor, finance, operations, technology, delivery, risk, and user scrutiny.

## Composite interpretation

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 32–35 | Executive-ready |
| 27–31 | Strong draft; one focused revision likely sufficient |
| 21–26 | Working draft; significant gaps remain |
| Below 21 | Structural rebuild needed |

## Review posture

The review should ask:

- Is the problem real, quantified, and urgent?
- Is the recommendation the result of honest option analysis?
- Are financial claims hard, soft, or assumed?
- What would a skeptical CFO challenge?
- What would an operational owner say is unrealistic?
- What would technology leadership say is underestimated?
- What decision is being requested, by whom, and by when?
