# Business Case Quality Rubric

Score each dimension from 1 to 5.

| Dimension | 5 means | Threshold before external use |
|---|---|---|
| Problem clarity | Problem stated in business terms, root cause separated from symptom, impact quantified, scope clear, urgency real | 4 |
| Evidence grounding | Quantitative claims trace to sources or assumptions; limitations acknowledged | 4 |
| Options integrity | Do Nothing and real alternatives evaluated against consistent criteria | 4 for high-stakes cases |
| Financial rigor | Complete cost model, hard/soft benefits separated, ROI/payback shown, sensitivity included | 4 |
| Risk honesty | Named risks, likelihood, impact, mitigation, residual risk, and owner | 4 |
| Executive readability | Summary stands alone; recommendation, financials, risk, and ask are easy to find | 4 |
| Decision clarity | Decision, decision-maker, deadline, consequence of delay, and next steps are explicit | 4 |

## Anti-patterns

### Solution-first framing

Weak: “We need a new PSA system.”

Corrective move: restate the business problem the tool is intended to solve.

### Quantification avoidance

Weak: “Significant delays.”

Corrective move: provide a directional estimate with assumptions if exact data is unavailable.

### Strawman alternative

Weak: one preferred option, one unrealistic option, and Do Nothing with no cost.

Corrective move: give each option a fair case and evaluate consistently.

### Soft benefits in ROI

Weak: including employee sentiment or brand value in the financial headline.

Corrective move: separate soft benefits from hard financial benefits.

### Mitigation platitude

Weak: “Communicate effectively.”

Corrective move: state a specific action, owner, cadence, and trigger.

### Vague call to action

Weak: “Leadership should consider moving forward.”

Corrective move: ask a named decision-maker to approve a named action by a named date.
