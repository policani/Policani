# Senior Portfolio Manager Quality Review

## Overall readiness rating

Ready with caveats.

The scoring model is usable for an initial portfolio board discussion, but the output should not be used as a final funding recommendation without resolving governance ownership for P-004 and validating cost/capacity assumptions across the portfolio.

## Strengths

- The model separates mandatory work from discretionary ranking, which prevents compliance work from being treated like ordinary preference-based prioritization.
- Criteria are plain-language and cover strategy, value, impact, risk, dependency enablement, readiness, and feasibility.
- Weights total 100% and are easy to explain.
- The generated output flags missing sponsor and decision authority rather than quietly scoring through the gap.
- The scenario view is useful because it tests budget and capacity pressure instead of ranking in isolation.

## Weaknesses

- The synthetic data uses directional estimates, not validated finance or delivery baselines.
- P-003 may need portfolio-board authority if central funding or shared delivery capacity is required; Operations Council authority may be insufficient.
- P-004 has strong dependency-enablement potential, but sponsor absence makes it unsuitable for funding decision.
- The model includes effort feasibility as a weighted criterion, but cost and capacity should still be reviewed separately to avoid hiding large delivery burden.
- Benefits are summarized qualitatively; no initiative has validated ROI, NPV, run-rate savings, revenue lift, or quantified risk exposure reduction.

## Material risks

| Risk | Impact | Recommended response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mandatory compliance work crowds out discretionary work | Strategic growth or efficiency work may slip. | Protect mandatory capacity first, then run scenario trade-offs for remaining capacity. |
| Scores may look more precise than the data supports | Board may over-trust the ranked list. | Show confidence and evidence notes next to scores. |
| Missing decision rights | Governance stalls or shadow decisions emerge. | Require sponsor and decision authority before funding decisions. |
| Dependency overload | Sequencing may fail even if funding is approved. | Review dependencies as a portfolio, not initiative-by-initiative. |

## Required corrections before executive review

1. Validate cost and capacity estimates with finance and delivery owners.
2. Confirm regulatory deadline and audit response date for P-001.
3. Confirm whether P-003 requires Portfolio Board approval or Operations Council approval is sufficient.
4. Name a sponsor and decision authority for P-004 or keep it on hold.
5. Add benefit quantification for each initiative before final funding decisions.

## Optional improvements

- Add benefit ranges and confidence intervals for financial-value scoring.
- Add a portfolio capacity reserve policy.
- Add a quarterly model-calibration session.
- Add a dependency map once real data contains more initiatives.
- Track score movement over time to identify churn or governance drift.

## Decision-board questions

1. Which capacity is already committed to mandatory work?
2. Is the board willing to fund P-002 before the identity and billing dependencies are ready?
3. Is P-003 a better near-term efficiency play under the current constraint scenario?
4. Who owns the business outcome for P-004?
5. What score confidence threshold is required for funding approval?
