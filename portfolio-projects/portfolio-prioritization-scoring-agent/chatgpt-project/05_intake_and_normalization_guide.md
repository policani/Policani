# Intake Logic

## Intake objective

The intake process captures enough information to evaluate portfolio fit and decision readiness without recreating an entire business case or charter.

## Minimum viable initiative fields

| Field | Required | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Initiative ID | Yes | Unique tracking key. |
| Title | Yes | Plain-language initiative name. |
| Category | Yes | Portfolio grouping. |
| Strategic theme | Yes | Strategy linkage. |
| Mandatory status | Yes | Mandatory, discretionary, committed, or unknown. |
| Sponsor | Yes | Accountable business leader. |
| Owner | Yes | Accountable initiative owner. |
| Decision authority | Yes | Body or person with approval authority. |
| Estimated cost | Preferred | Funding trade-off view. |
| Capacity estimate | Preferred | Sequencing and feasibility view. |
| Benefit summary | Preferred | Value basis. |
| Risk rating | Preferred | Portfolio risk distribution. |
| Dependencies | Preferred | Sequencing and cross-initiative risk. |
| Criterion scores | Yes after scoring | Weighted scoring inputs. |
| Evidence notes | Preferred | Audit trail for score rationale. |

## Intake states

| State | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| New | Item captured but not triaged. |
| Needs clarification | Required fields are missing or conflicting. |
| Ready for scoring | Minimum data is complete enough to score. |
| Scored | Weighted score and flags generated. |
| Ready for board review | Decision-support brief prepared. |
| Decision recorded | Human decision and rationale captured. |

## Missing-data flags

Flag the item when any of the following are missing or unclear:

- Sponsor
- Owner
- Decision authority
- Mandatory status
- Strategic theme
- Estimated cost
- Capacity estimate
- Benefit claim
- Major dependency
- Regulatory or contractual deadline

## Strategy and constraint inputs

Before scoring, collect:

- Strategic themes and definitions
- Portfolio categories
- Budget cap by period
- Capacity cap by period
- Mandatory-work policy
- Governance cadence
- Decision rights
- Known delivery constraints
- Known excluded work types

## Scoring readiness rule

An initiative can be scored with incomplete data, but it should not be treated as decision-ready unless sponsor, owner, decision authority, mandatory status, cost range, capacity range, and benefit basis are visible or explicitly waived by governance.
