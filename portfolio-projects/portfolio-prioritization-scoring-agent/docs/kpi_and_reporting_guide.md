# Portfolio KPIs and Reporting Guide

## Reporting purpose

Portfolio reporting should help leaders understand allocation, trade-offs, overload, risk, and decision needs. It should not create status theater or hide uncertainty behind summary colors.

## Recommended portfolio KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Spend by strategic theme | Percent of estimated spend mapped to each theme. | Shows whether funding follows strategy. |
| Mandatory vs discretionary mix | Percent of initiatives or spend by mandatory status. | Shows how much capacity is already constrained. |
| Capacity demand by category | FTE-months or team capacity required by category. | Exposes overload and sequencing pressure. |
| Risk distribution | Count or spend by risk rating. | Shows aggregate portfolio exposure. |
| Decision-readiness rate | Percent of initiatives with sponsor, owner, decision authority, cost, capacity, and benefit basis. | Shows governance hygiene. |
| Dependency density | Count of initiatives with dependencies and high dependency chains. | Shows sequencing risk and systemic coupling. |
| Score confidence mix | Percent of initiatives scored High, Medium, Low confidence. | Prevents weak assumptions from looking final. |
| Board decision backlog | Count of initiatives needing funding, sequencing, deferral, or escalation decisions. | Keeps governance focused on decisions. |

## Summary views

The standard decision-support brief should include:

1. Executive summary
2. Portfolio composition
3. Ranked discretionary initiatives
4. Mandatory commitments
5. Budget and capacity view
6. Risk and dependency view
7. Missing data and governance issues
8. Scenario options
9. Human decisions required
10. Decision log reference

## Chart descriptions for Markdown or HTML

Use simple, explainable chart views:

- Bar chart: weighted score by initiative
- Stacked bar: spend by strategic theme
- Donut or table: mandatory versus discretionary mix
- Heat table: risk rating by category
- Dependency table: initiative, dependency, blocking impact, owner

If generating charts programmatically is not available, use tables and plain-language chart descriptions rather than fabricating visuals.
