# Portfolio Operating Model

## Purpose

The operating model defines how initiatives move from intake to scoring, review, decision support, and ongoing portfolio monitoring. It is intentionally lightweight so it can be adopted by business, technology, operations, and product teams without requiring enterprise PPM tooling on day one.

## Portfolio flow

1. **Intake:** Capture initiative metadata and attach or summarize upstream artifacts.
2. **Triage:** Confirm category, mandatory status, sponsor, owner, decision authority, cost range, capacity estimate, benefits, risks, and dependencies.
3. **Design-time calibration:** Confirm scoring criteria, weights, scale anchors, and mandatory-work policy.
4. **Run-time scoring:** Score each initiative using the agreed model.
5. **Quality review:** Check completeness, evidence, assumptions, and confidence.
6. **Scenario analysis:** Test budget, capacity, sequencing, dependency, or strategy-weight changes.
7. **Decision-support brief:** Present options and decisions required from accountable leaders.
8. **Decision logging:** Record the human decision, rationale, conditions, and follow-ups.
9. **Monitoring:** Track portfolio KPIs, changes, and decision outcomes.

## Portfolio governance cadence

| Cadence | Meeting | Purpose | Output |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Weekly | Intake hygiene review | Validate new items and missing fields | Intake exceptions list |
| Biweekly | Scoring review | Review score changes, assumptions, and conflicts | Updated scorecard and log |
| Monthly | Portfolio board | Make funding, sequencing, deferral, or escalation decisions | Decision record and approved view |
| Quarterly | Strategy and model calibration | Revisit criteria, weights, categories, and KPIs | Updated model and operating assumptions |

## Decision rights

The model should clarify who can make each decision:

| Decision | Recommended decision owner | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Accept item into portfolio intake | Portfolio manager or intake owner | This is not approval to fund. |
| Classify mandatory status | Sponsor with legal, risk, finance, or compliance input | Evidence required for mandatory classification. |
| Approve criteria and weights | Portfolio board | Should be reviewed quarterly or when strategy changes. |
| Approve funding | Accountable executive or portfolio board | Must align with finance authority. |
| Approve sequencing | Portfolio board with delivery leadership | Must consider capacity and dependencies. |
| Cancel or defer initiative | Accountable decision owner | Record rationale and impact. |

## Roles

| Role | Responsibilities |
| --- | --- |
| Portfolio Sponsor | Owns strategic outcomes and benefit realization. |
| Portfolio Manager | Runs intake, scoring, reporting, dependency visibility, and decision support. |
| Initiative Sponsor | Owns business case, priority, and benefit claim. |
| Initiative Owner | Owns delivery approach, scope clarity, and status inputs. |
| Finance Partner | Validates cost, affordability, capitalization assumptions, and benefit logic. |
| Technology / Architecture Partner | Validates technical feasibility, risks, constraints, and dependencies. |
| Operations Partner | Validates operating impact, change burden, process ownership, and readiness. |

## Practical operating principle

The scoring model should make trade-offs easier to discuss, not harder. If stakeholders cannot explain why one initiative scores above another, the model is too opaque or too poorly calibrated.
