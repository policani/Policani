# Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Portfolio | A group of projects, programs, or other work managed together to support strategic objectives. |
| Initiative | A project, program, product effort, or major work item being considered in the portfolio. |
| Weighted scoring | A prioritization method where initiatives are rated against criteria and each criterion has a weight. |
| Mandatory work | Work required by legal, regulatory, safety, security, contractual, audit, or executive commitment. |
| Discretionary work | Work that can be sequenced, funded, deferred, reshaped, or declined based on strategy and constraints. |
| Strategic theme | A declared business priority used to classify portfolio value. |
| Decision authority | Person or governance body with authority to approve, fund, defer, or cancel work. |
| Sponsor | Accountable leader who owns the business value or outcome. |
| Owner | Person accountable for progressing the initiative through delivery or governance. |
| Dependency | A relationship where one initiative depends on another decision, deliverable, resource, platform, or timing condition. |
| Confidence flag | A High, Medium, or Low indicator showing how well the score is supported by evidence. |
| Decision-support brief | Executive-ready summary of options, trade-offs, risks, and decisions required. |
