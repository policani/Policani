# PMBOK® Guide-Aligned Positioning

Project Charter Initiation Agent uses recognizable project initiation terminology and practices commonly associated with PMI/PMBOK-style project management.

The package is centered on the project charter as the formal initiation artifact that establishes:

- Project authorization
- Sponsor and owner accountability
- Business-case linkage
- High-level scope boundaries
- Authorized deliverables
- Success measures
- Assumptions and constraints
- Initial risks and dependencies
- Governance rhythm and escalation path
- Change-control expectations
- Transition from initiation into planning and execution

## What the charter is not

The charter is not a replacement for the business case, project management plan, backlog, work breakdown structure, technical design, implementation plan, or detailed budget baseline.

The business case explains why the project may be worth doing. The charter authorizes what will be done, who owns it, what boundaries apply, how decisions will be made, and what success will mean.

## Wording guidance

Use “PMBOK® Guide-aligned” or “PMI-style project initiation” when describing the package. Avoid language that implies official PMI certification, sponsorship, endorsement, or compliance validation.
