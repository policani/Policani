# Sample Business Case Input

## Scenario

A fictional regional utility services company has field technicians who complete maintenance and repair work across distributed service territories. Dispatch, parts readiness, and technician scheduling are managed in separate tools and spreadsheets.

## Problem hypothesis

Technicians lose productive time because dispatch assignments are made before parts readiness and travel sequencing are confirmed.

## Directional data

- 120 technicians
- Average 6.5 lost productive hours per technician per month
- Loaded technician cost: $82/hour
- Emergency reschedules: 18% of work orders
- Parts-not-ready delay rate: 14% of planned work orders
- Proposed program investment: $620,000 one-time and $180,000 recurring annually
- Estimated hard benefit: $1.25M annually
- Expected payback: under 12 months

## Audience

Primary audience: COO and CFO. Secondary audience: field operations and IT leadership.

## Decision requested

Approve a phased dispatch and parts readiness modernization program.
