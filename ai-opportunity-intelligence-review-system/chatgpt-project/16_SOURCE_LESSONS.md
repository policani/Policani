# Source Lessons

Derived lessons from the supplied video research:

- More automation can create more review, orchestration, and context-maintenance work. Evaluate the human operating model, not just the automated task.
- Agents need scaffolding: reusable instructions, tools, data access, scripts, connectors, skills, logs, evals, and human review points.
- AI investment is a work-shape question before it is a model, vendor, or dashboard question.
- The intelligence loop can be decomposed into purpose, sensing, interpretation, decision, orchestration, learning, and governance.
- Governance needs evals, searchable logs, rollback, permissions, and human review queues.
- Proof matters. AI claims should connect to evidence, baselines, examples, and agent-readable truth layers.
- Avoid AI washing. Clear capability evidence is stronger than broad AI-native language.
- The edge of useful AI is where the model meets a real human workflow.
