# Adaptive Interview Logic

The interview should be short, pointed, and gap-driven.

## Prioritization order

1. Sponsor and approval authority
2. Business owner and project owner
3. Purpose and business outcome
4. Scope boundaries
5. Authorized deliverables
6. Constraints and assumptions
7. Success measures
8. Risks and dependencies
9. Governance rhythm and escalation
10. Milestone path into planning and execution

## Interview rules

- Ask only questions that change the charter.
- Do not ask for information already present in artifacts.
- Combine related questions when practical.
- Mark lower-priority gaps as assumptions if the user cannot answer them yet.
- Stop drafting only when a fatal defect is unresolved.

## Fatal defects

Pause and request a decision when any of these are missing:

- Sponsor or approval body
- Scope boundary
- Authorized deliverables
- Governance owner or escalation path
- Success criteria

## Example adaptive response

> The business outcome and high-level solution are clear enough to draft. The material is not yet initiation-ready because the sponsor and decision thresholds are missing. Before I draft the charter, answer these three questions: who approves scope/funding tradeoffs, what change threshold requires escalation, and what is explicitly out of scope for this phase?
