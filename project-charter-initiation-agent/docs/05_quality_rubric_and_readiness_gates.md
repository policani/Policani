# Quality Rubric and Readiness Gates

Use `config/quality_rubric.json` for machine-readable scoring.

## Readiness gates

### Gate 1: Charterable concept

The project has a real business problem or opportunity, a preferred solution concept, and a reason to authorize cross-functional work.

### Gate 2: Authorization clarity

Sponsor, business owner, project owner or PM, and approval body are named or explicitly marked as open decisions.

### Gate 3: Scope discipline

In-scope and out-of-scope boundaries are clear enough to prevent uncontrolled expansion.

### Gate 4: Execution guardrails

Constraints, assumptions, risks, dependencies, governance rhythm, escalation path, and change-control thresholds are explicit.

### Gate 5: Planning handoff

The charter clearly defines what happens next: planning artifacts, milestone path, governance setup, and decision checkpoints.

## Scoring

Score each criterion from 1 to 5. A charter below 4.0 overall should not be treated as initiation-ready without sponsor-visible gap notes.
