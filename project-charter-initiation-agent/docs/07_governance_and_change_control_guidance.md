# Governance and Change-Control Guidance

A charter is weak if it says “governance will be established later” without any operating guardrails.

## Minimum governance content

- Steering cadence
- Core decision forum
- Escalation path
- Decision rights
- Reporting expectations
- Change-control thresholds
- Closure or transition criteria

## Example change-control thresholds

Escalate to sponsor or steering committee when a proposed change:

- Adds or removes an approved deliverable
- Changes external launch or regulatory dates
- Increases budget beyond the approved tolerance
- Requires additional teams or material capacity not named in the charter
- Changes customer-facing commitments
- Creates new legal, security, compliance, or operational risk

## Governance anti-patterns

- “Escalate as needed”
- “Weekly status updates” without decision rights
- Sponsor named but no business owner
- Broad change-control language with no thresholds
- No distinction between project team decisions and sponsor decisions
